The FlowStable ecosystem
Five products. One mental model.

We are building a coherent suite instead of a pile of disconnected tools. Each piece can stand alone, but they make the most sense together.

Software wallet
Your primary money interface.

A desktop‑grade, self‑custodial wallet designed for people who live in crypto — balances, activity, and controls in one calm surface.

Non‑custodial by default. No “sign in with email”.

Hardware wallet
Cold storage without friction.

A hardware device for long‑term holdings that still plays nicely with your day‑to‑day software wallet.

Designed to feel like part of the same product, not a separate brand.

Dripper
Streaming money instead of batches.

A primitive for salaries, subscriptions, and recurring transfers that move every second instead of once a month.

Built for employers, contributors, and protocols.

FlowStable card
Spend from your stack in the real world.

A card experience that respects self‑custody — so funding comes from your wallet, not a black‑box account.

Planned: online, in‑store, and virtual numbers.

FlowStable SDK
A developer kit that lets you plug FlowStable primitives into your own products — without forcing your users into a new custodian.
Wallets, Dripper, card, and account logic exposed as clear, composable building blocks.

Questions
How FlowStable treats your money.
Is FlowStable a bank or an exchange? +
FlowStable is a self‑custodial wallet with a card and account‑like features. You keep control of your keys; we orchestrate UX, not custody.
Can I always withdraw my funds? +
Yes. Your assets live in wallets you control. Even if FlowStable goes away, you can move funds using your keys and standard tooling.
Where does yield come from? +
Yield comes from on‑chain protocols and counterparties. We route and monitor; you can see the sources, adjust allocations, or opt‑out entirely.
Who is FlowStable for? +
Crypto‑native people and teams who want a serious, programmable home for their money — without handing it to a custodian.
