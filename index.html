How it works

Card payments from
a non-custodial wallet.

When you swipe your Tranzo card, the system translates it into a wallet transaction — settled through normal payment rails.

01

Hold your keys

Funds live in your non-custodial wallet. Private keys never leave your device.

02

Swipe or tap

Use the Tranzo card at any POS terminal or online checkout. Works like a debit card.

03

Sign locally

Your wallet signs the transaction on-device. The platform relays — never controls.

04

Settled

The backend converts the wallet transaction and settles with the merchant via card networks.

FAQ

Common questions.

Is Tranzo a bank or an exchange? +
Neither. Tranzo is a self-custodial wallet with card and account-like features. You keep control of your keys — we orchestrate the UX, not custody.
Can I always withdraw my funds? +
Yes. Your assets live in wallets you control. Even if Tranzo disappears, you can move funds using your keys and standard tooling. No withdrawal limits, ever.
How does the card work without custody? +
When the card is used, the backend triggers a transaction from your wallet. You sign locally — the platform acts as a relay and settlement layer, never holding your funds.
Where does yield come from? +
Yield comes from on-chain protocols and counterparties. We route and monitor — you can see the sources, adjust allocations, or opt out entirely.
Who is Tranzo for? +
Crypto-native people and teams who want a serious, programmable home for their money — without handing it to a custodian.
Can I use Tranzo offline? +
We're building optional offline payment support using telecom channels like USSD and IVR, so feature phones can still initiate transactions without internet.
